Biography

I’m Ramin Nabati, currently a PhD student in Electrical Engineering at The University of Tennessee Knoxville (UTK) advised by Dr. Hairong Qi. My research focuses on object detection and sensor fusion for autonomous vehicles. I’m also the Connected and Automated Vehicles (CAV) team lead for Team Tennessee in the EcoCAR Mobility Challenge competition, tasked with design, simulation and implementation of a Level 2 autonomous driving system for a 2019 Chevrolet Blazer.

Projects

CenterFusion

A center-based radar and camera fusion for 3D object detection in autonomous vehicles.

Radar-Camera Sensor Fusion and Depth Estimation

A novel radar-camera sensor fusion framework for accurate object detection and distance estimation in autonomous vehicles.

Radar Region Proposal Network

Radar Region Proposal Network (RRPN) uses Radar detections in an autonomous vehicle to generate real-time region proposals for two-stage object detection networks.

EcoCAR Mobility Challenge

The EcoCAR Mobility Challenge will task 12 North American universities to apply advanced propulsion systems, electrification, SAE Level 2 automation, and vehicle connectivity to improve the energy efficiency of a 2019 Chevrolet Blazer.

EcoCAR 3

EcoCAR3 is challenging 16 North American university teams to redesign a 2016 Chevrolet Camaro. The ADAS team focuses on integrating the sensing system on the Camaro and deploy driver feedback to improve efficiency and safety.

Spacenet 3: Road Network Detection

The Spacenet 3 challenge is focused on determining road networks and routing information directly from satellite imagery. The SpaceNet 3 Dataset contains ~8,000 km of roads across the four SpaceNet Areas of Interest.
